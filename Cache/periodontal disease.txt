#Header: Introduction to Periodontal Disease
#Content: 
- Periodontal disease is a chronic inflammatory condition affecting the gums and supporting structures of the teeth.
- It is caused by bacteria in dental plaque, leading to inflammation and destruction of the tissues.
- Common symptoms include gum bleeding, bad breath, and tooth loss if left untreated.

#Slide: 3
#Header: Relationship between Periodontal Disease and Cardiovascular Health
#Content: 
- Research suggests a bidirectional relationship between periodontal disease and cardiovascular health.
- Chronic inflammation in periodontal disease may contribute to the development and progression of cardiovascular diseases.
- Studies have shown associations between periodontal disease and conditions like heart disease, stroke, and atherosclerosis.

#Slide: 4
#Header: Health Professionals' Knowledge and Clinical Practice
#Content: 
- A study conducted in León, Spain, aimed to assess health professionals' knowledge and clinical practice regarding the relationship between cardiovascular diseases and periodontal disease.
- The study found that there was a lack of awareness and knowledge among health professionals about this relationship.
- It highlighted the need for improved education and collaboration between dental and medical professionals.

#Slide: 5
#Header: Conclusion
#Content: 
- Periodontal disease is a chronic inflammatory condition affecting the gums and supporting structures of the teeth.
- There is a growing body of evidence suggesting a link between periodontal disease and cardiovascular health.
- Health professionals need to be educated about this relationship to provide comprehensive care to their patients.
